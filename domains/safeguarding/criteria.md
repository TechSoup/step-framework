# Safeguarding — Assessment Criteria

This domain assesses whether the CSO protects vulnerable populations,
implements child protection measures, and maintains safe recruitment practices.

---

## Tier 1 — Basic (2 minimum requirements)

- **SAF-B01**: Demonstrates a basic understanding/awareness of safeguarding and showing that it has taken some steps to support this internally, as well as for actually treating its beneficiaries and other vulnerable populations with respect and avoid doing them harm or putting them in a position where they could be harmed and have no history of failing to do so, without a legitimate explanation.
- **SAF-B02**: Has some mechanism to collect complaints from the community, whether by email, website, feedback box, etc.

---

## Tier 2 — Foundation (5 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1 requirements, plus:*

- **SAF-F01**: Has a process/guidance for both documenting and securely storing incidences of alleged abuse, concerns, or referrals that embeds victim/survivor-centered principles.
- **SAF-F02**: Is able to articulate a position or policy on safeguarding, if not in a formal policy.
- **SAF-F03**: Has a code of conduct in some form, which may be in the form of a Bullying/Harassment Policy, Code of Ethics, etc.
- **SAF-F04**: Has a policy or practice of safe recruitment — meaning it either has a written policy in place, or is able to articulate how it ensures staff working with vulnerable populations are properly vetted.
- **SAF-F05**: Prohibits unsupervised contact between its representatives (staff, volunteers, contractors, partners, etc.) and vulnerable populations.

---

## Tier 3 — Agency (6 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-2 requirements, plus:*

- **SAF-A01**: Has a safeguarding policy in place that embeds survivor-centered principles (must also incorporate child protection if the organization's staff or personnel work with children — may also be a separate policy).
- **SAF-A02**: Conducts background checks on all staff and volunteers who may be exposed to vulnerable populations.
- **SAF-A03**: Has some method of ensuring that it abides by The UN Convention of the Rights of the Child (UNCRC) (*if it works with/for children*).
- **SAF-A04**: Has a clear, straightforward mechanism for children to report abuse within the organization (*if the org works with children*).
- **SAF-A05**: Has a means to conduct safeguarding risk assessments (even informally) for its programs that involve vulnerable populations.
- **SAF-A06**: Requires that all of its partners, contractors, subgrantees, or other affiliates that may be exposed to vulnerable populations, be subject to the same policies, restrictions, and training regarding such populations.

---

## Tier 4 — Plus (3 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-3 requirements, plus:*

- **SAF-P01**: Has a designated staff person or committee whose role is to monitor and ensure that the organization's processes around safeguarding (with respect to children as well as other vulnerable populations) is consistently and appropriately implemented.
- **SAF-P02**: Provides training and development opportunities for staff that work with children, specific to the needs and sensitivities of children in different cultures and contexts.
- **SAF-P03**: Conducts a safeguarding risk assessment for each program that entails working with vulnerable populations, with a plan for the integration of safeguarding mitigations in program design and implementation.

---

## Red Flags

- **Possible red flag** if they have received complaints regarding their treatment of vulnerable populations, *unless there was an adequate response to prevent it from recurring.*
