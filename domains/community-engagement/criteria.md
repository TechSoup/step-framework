# Commitment to Community Engagement — Assessment Criteria

This domain assesses whether the CSO delivers aid impartially, engages the
communities it serves, and maintains transparent, inclusive practices.

---

## Tier 1 — Basic (1 minimum requirement)

- **CCE-B01**: Delivers aid impartially and has no history of failing to do so, without a legitimate explanation.

---

## Tier 2 — Foundation (4 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1 requirements, plus:*

- **CCE-F01**: Has some process in place to ensure that aid is delivered impartially.
- **CCE-F02**: Has a process for ensuring that its programs are implemented by staff with appropriate expertise.
- **CCE-F03**: Has some process by which it learns from prior mistakes, and implements changes based on feedback, monitoring, and evaluation.
- **CCE-F04**: Takes some measures to ensure that it is working for the affected communities in a way that is transparent, participatory, and inclusive.

---

## Tier 3 — Agency (3 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-2 requirements, plus:*

- **CCE-A01**: Has a written anti-discrimination policy or process.
- **CCE-A02**: Enters into written agreements for all anticipated collaborations with partners that detail relevant responsibilities, liabilities, and protections of all parties involved.
- **CCE-A03**: Implements and/or commits to at least one relevant humanitarian aid standard/principle.

---

## Tier 4 — Plus (2 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-3 requirements, plus:*

- **CCE-P01**: Adequately monitors the actions of local and/or international partners to ensure they are not operating in a way that violates the organization's own ethical and safeguarding policies.
- **CCE-P02**: Is a member of at least one coordination body, network, council, or other group that brings together voices and expertise of different constituents.
