# Risk Management — Assessment Criteria

This domain assesses whether the CSO identifies, understands, and manages the
risks it faces — including corruption, conflict, security, and financial risks.

---

## Tier 1 — Basic (1 minimum requirement)

- **RSK-B01**: Demonstrates some understanding of the kind of risks it faces whether in terms of corruption, conflict, natural disaster, etc. If it doesn't have any written policies or practices in place, it must at a minimum be able to articulate how it would propose to address such risks.

---

## Tier 2 — Foundation (1 minimum requirement)

*All Tier 1 requirements, plus:*

- **RSK-F01**: Has a mechanism in place to confidentially receive and handle reports of abuse, fraud, bribery, or other wrongdoing from both external and internal parties.

---

## Tier 3 — Agency (8 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-2 requirements, plus:*

- **RSK-A01**: Has an anti-money laundering policy (or equivalent).
- **RSK-A02**: Has an anti-bribery policy (or equivalent).
- **RSK-A03**: Has an anti-corruption policy (or equivalent).
- **RSK-A04**: Has some kind of emergency policy or procedure in place that will dictate how best to protect its personnel, assets, and beneficiaries in the event of the unexpected.
- **RSK-A05**: Has some mechanism in place to address "high-risk" regions and situations, either in terms of physical safety or risk of asset diversion.
- **RSK-A06**: Has some method of ensuring that it is not funding terrorist individuals or activities.

---

## Tier 4 — Plus (5 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-3 requirements, plus:*

- **RSK-P01**: Has a risk management policy (or equivalent).
- **RSK-P02**: Maintains a risk register.
- **RSK-P03**: Has an anti-terrorism policy (or equivalent).
- **RSK-P04**: Maintains some kind of insurance.
- **RSK-P05**: Has at least two different methods of addressing "high-risk" regions and situations, either in terms of physical safety or risk of asset diversion.
