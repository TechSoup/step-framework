# Data Security and Privacy — Assessment Criteria

This domain assesses whether the CSO takes adequate steps to protect data,
ensure cybersecurity, and maintain privacy of sensitive information.

---

## Tier 1 — Basic (1 minimum requirement)

- **DSP-B01**: Has some method of securing such data, whether in a closed file cabinet or in a password-protected shared drive, for example.

---

## Tier 2 — Foundation (1 minimum requirement)

*All Tier 1 requirements, plus:*

- **DSP-F01**: Has password protections on any organizational systems or devices.

---

## Tier 3 — Agency (5 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-2 requirements, plus:*

- **DSP-A01**: Has a Privacy Policy (or equivalents) in place.
- **DSP-A02**: Has a Data Protection or Security Policy (or equivalents) in place.
- **DSP-A03**: Has a dedicated staff person or committee in charge of ensuring and monitoring cybersecurity.
- **DSP-A04**: Takes a minimum of three cybersecurity steps on all devices/users (e.g., passwords, firewalls, automatic updates, anti-malware installed, cybersecurity training, etc.), one of which must include back-ups (in addition to the password requirement for Tier 2).
- **DSP-A05**: Takes some measure to ensure that personal data collected from donors, beneficiaries, staff, volunteers, and others are protected.

---

## Tier 4 — Plus (2 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-3 requirements, plus:*

- **DSP-P01**: Has a dedicated staff person or committee in charge of ensuring and monitoring data privacy.
- **DSP-P02**: Takes a minimum of five cybersecurity steps on all devices/users (e.g., passwords, firewalls, automatic updates, anti-malware installed, cybersecurity training, etc.).

---

## Red Flags

- **Possible red flag** for having experienced a significant data breach within the past five years, *unless there was an adequate response to prevent it from recurring.*
