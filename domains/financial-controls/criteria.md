# Financial Controls — Assessment Criteria

This domain assesses whether the CSO maintains adequate financial management
practices, including accounting, budgeting, auditing, and asset protection.

---

## Tier 1 — Basic (2 minimum requirements)

- **FIN-B01**: Maintains its funds in a bank account in the name of the organization, or having an adequate explanation for why it doesn't, and how it otherwise protects its assets.*
- **FIN-B02**: Has no past incidents of fraud or asset misuse without a legitimate explanation and/or correction action taken.

> *Requirement may be waived if organization satisfies additional assurance questions (AAQs) providing a legitimate basis for its variance from this requirement.

---

## Tier 2 — Foundation (5 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1 requirements, plus:*

- **FIN-F01**: Has annual audited accounts by an independent auditor **OR**, if no audited accounts, then has for two years of financial statements that have both been prepared by an individual with financial expertise of some sort (e.g., past experience in similar roles, or relevant education), and that have been approved by a separate individual or body of the organization.
- **FIN-F02**: Records and maintains receipts for all expenditures.
- **FIN-F03**: Has a policy or written process regarding the distribution, tracking, and maintenance of cash on hand.
- **FIN-F04**: Prepares an annual budget.
- **FIN-F05**: Tracks expenses against this budget.

---

## Tier 3 — Agency (7 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-2 requirements, plus:*

- **FIN-A01**: Has written policies or processes regarding expense reimbursements and asset use and disposal.
- **FIN-A02**: Has written guidance on recording and valuing contributions in kind.
- **FIN-A03**: Has at least one individual officer and/or staff whose responsibilities include preparing financial accounts, monitoring finances, and/or working with outside parties to prepare and manage financial accounts, AND who has relevant expertise.
- **FIN-A04**: Conducts periodic inventory counts that include reconciling the lists with available updates.
- **FIN-A05**: Has a written policy or procedure regarding who is permitted to sign checks and at what amounts, including when two or more signatures may be required.
- **FIN-A06**: Ensures that the organization's controls are not all vested in a single individual, which may be evidenced by dual check-signing requirements, or enough staff with clear divided responsibilities over funds.
- **FIN-A07**: Has some method of guiding the organization's hiring and contracting practices that aims to prevent conflicts of interest or significant losses, and help ensure value for money.

---

## Tier 4 — Plus (4 minimum requirements)

*All Tier 1-3 requirements, plus:*

- **FIN-P01**: Has some kind of travel policy/procedure and procurement policy/procedure.
- **FIN-P02**: Maintains a separate account for all inventory.
- **FIN-P03**: Has 2 or more individual officers and/or staff whose responsibilities include preparing financial accounts, monitoring finances, and/or working with outside parties to prepare and manage financial accounts.
- **FIN-P04**: Has a written policy and also implementing a clear segregation of duties regarding cash, assets, and bank accounts that ensures no one individual can (without approval or oversight) withdraw or transfer more than minimal amounts of the organization's assets.

---

## Red Flags

- **Possible red flag** if organization has been required to return funds to a donor within the past five years, *unless there was an adequate response to prevent it from recurring.*
- **Possible red flag** if audited financials show any serious misconduct or misuse of funds, *unless there was an adequate reason/explanation.*
