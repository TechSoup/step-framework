# Glossary

Definitions used throughout the STEP framework (Strengthening and Tiered Evaluation Process).
Where terminology varies by region or legal system, the variation is noted.

---

**Assessment**
A structured evaluation of a CSO against STEP criteria at a defined tier,
conducted by a certified evaluator. Produces an Assessment Report and domain scores.

**Civil Society Organization (CSO)**
A non-governmental, non-commercial organization operating in the public interest.
Includes NGOs, nonprofits, community-based organizations, associations, and foundations.
*Regional note: In some jurisdictions these may be called NGOs, NPOs, or charities.*

**Criterion**
A single, scoreable question or requirement within a domain. Each criterion has
an ID, tier applicability, scoring rubric, and evidence guidance.

**Domain**
A thematic grouping of criteria covering one area of organizational operation
(e.g., Governance, Financial Controls). STEP v0.1 assesses across 11 domains.
*Also referred to as "streams" by TechSoup — both terms are used interchangeably.*

**Due Diligence**
The process by which a funder verifies that a CSO is legitimate, well-governed,
and capable of managing resources responsibly before making a grant.

**Evaluator**
A trained, certified individual who reviews CSO evidence submissions and assigns
scores. STEP assessments are reviewed by **local or regional evaluators** — trained
practitioners who understand the operating context of the CSOs they evaluate.
Evaluators are certified by TechSoup or an approved regional partner organization.

**Funder**
Any entity providing grants or contracts to CSOs — foundations, governments,
bilateral donors, or corporations. Funders use STEP assessment reports to
inform funding decisions.

**Red Flag**
A condition in a domain that warrants additional scrutiny regardless of the
criterion score. Red flags trigger additional assurance questions (AAQs).

**Stream**
See *Domain*.

**Tier**
The assessment level assigned to a CSO within a given domain. Tiers are applied
per domain — a CSO may receive different tier ratings across different domains,
reflecting real variation in organizational maturity.
Tier 1 (Basic) → Tier 2 (Foundation) → Tier 3 (Agency) → Tier 4 (Plus).
See [tiered-framework.md](tiered-framework.md).
